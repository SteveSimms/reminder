What is the Reminder?

The Reminder refers to the teachings and messages from Allah, particularly those conveyed through the Quran and the Prophets. It serves as guidance, a warning, and a reminder of Allah’s mercy and the importance of faith and righteousness for humanity.

What is the Quran?

The Quran is the holy book of Islam, believed to be the word of Allah (God) as revealed to the Prophet Muhammad through the Angel Gabriel. It serves as a guide for Muslims, containing teachings, laws, and a comprehensive framework for life, encompassing spirituality, morality, and guidance for personal conduct. The Quran is written in Arabic and is known for its distinct style and structure.

What is the Hadith?

A Hadith is a report or narrative that documents the sayings, actions, or approvals of the Prophet Muhammad. It serves as a key source of guidance for Muslims in understanding Islamic teachings, alongside the Quran. Hadiths are classified as authentic (Sahih), fair (Hasan), and weak (Da’if) based on their chain of transmission and reliability.

Who is Allah to us?

Allah is the Creator of all things, the Most Compassionate and Most Merciful. He is the Knower of the seen and unseen, the Maintainer of everything, and the Supreme Judge. All authority belongs to Him, and to Him will all beings return. Allah is also a witness to our actions and provides guidance and sustenance to humanity. Ultimately, He is the One worthy of worship and devotion.

Who is the prophet Muhammad

Prophet Muhammad is considered the last messenger of Allah in Islam. Born in Mecca around 570 CE, he is revered for delivering the Quran, the holy book of Islam, and for his teachings promoting monotheism, moral conduct, and social justice. He is known for his compassion, leadership, and dedication to guiding his followers towards the worship of one God.

How do we ‘worship’ Allah?

Worshiping Allah involves a sincere devotion to Him, which includes following His commands, observing the five daily prayers, engaging in acts of charity, and displaying good character. It is essential to worship Allah alone without associating any partners with Him and to be grateful for His blessings.

Where did we come from?

According to Islamic teachings, humanity was created by Allah from humble materials such as clay and sperm-drop. Specifically, it is mentioned that humans were created from clay, an extract of clay, and a drop of mixed fluids, emphasizing the divine origin and purpose of creation.

Where do we go when we die?

When we die, we transition to the afterlife, where our fate is determined in accordance with our deeds in this world. Righteous individuals are believed to enter Paradise, while those who have done wrong may face consequences in Hell. Ultimately, everyone will be gathered before Allah for judgment.

How do I remember Allah?

You can remember Allah by engaging in regular prayers, reciting the Quran, and making dhikr (remembrance of Allah). Additionally, reflecting on His blessings and favors, being mindful of His presence in your life, and reciting specific supplications can help strengthen your remembrance of Him.
