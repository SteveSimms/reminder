What is the Reminder?

The Reminder refers to the revelations and guidance sent by Allah, primarily through the Quran, which serves as a warning, guidance, and mercy for humanity. It emphasizes the importance of remembering Allah and living a life in accordance with His teachings.

What is the Quran?

The Quran is the holy book of Islam, believed to be the word of Allah (God) as revealed to the Prophet Muhammad through the angel Gabriel. It serves as a guide for all aspects of life, containing teachings on worship, morality, and legal principles. It is written in Arabic and is regarded as the ultimate source of authority in Islam.

What is the Hadith?

Hadith refers to the recorded sayings, actions, and approvals of the Prophet Muhammad. It serves as a significant source of guidance in Islam, second only to the Quran, and provides insights into Islamic teachings, ethics, and law. Each Hadith consists of two parts: the chain of narrators (isnad) and the text (matn).

Who is Allah to us?

Allah is the unique Creator, Sustainer, and Lord of all that exists. He is the Most Compassionate, Most Merciful, all-Knowing, and all-Wise. Allah is the one worthy of worship and devotion, as He alone holds authority over the heavens and the earth.

Who is the prophet Muhammad

Prophet Muhammad is regarded as the last messenger of Allah in Islam. He was born in Mecca around 570 CE and is known for receiving divine revelations that form the basis of the Quran. He is recognized as a model for Muslims, embodying virtues like compassion, honesty, and justice. Muhammad’s role includes being a witness to the truth, a bearer of good news, and a warner. His teachings emphasize monotheism, moral conduct, and the importance of worshiping Allah.

How do we ‘worship’ Allah?

Worshiping Allah involves sincere devotion and submission to Him alone. This includes performing the five daily prayers, following His commandments as outlined in the Quran and Sunnah, expressing gratitude, engaging in acts of kindness, seeking forgiveness, and maintaining a strong connection through prayer and remembrance. Additionally, living a life aligned with Islamic principles and values demonstrates worship in action.

Where did we come from?

We were created from humble beginnings, specifically from dust and a sperm-drop, as described in the Quran.

Where do we go when we die?

In Islamic belief, when a person dies, their soul is taken by the Angel of Death, and they enter a stage called Barzakh, which is a state between death and resurrection. The deceased’s destination is determined by their deeds in life, and they will ultimately be judged by Allah on the Day of Judgment, leading them either to Paradise or Hell.

How do I remember Allah?

You can remember Allah by engaging in various acts of worship and mindfulness, such as:

  1. Prayers (Salah): Establish regular prayers throughout the day, as they serve as a direct connection with Allah.
  2. Dhikr: Recite phrases of remembrance, such as “SubhanAllah” (Glory be to Allah), “Alhamdulillah” (All praise be to Allah), and “Allahu Akbar” (Allah is the Greatest).
  3. Reading the Quran: Recite and reflect on the verses of the Quran regularly.
  4. Meditation and Reflection: Take moments for quiet reflection on Allah’s creation and His attributes.
  5. Thankfulness: Express gratitude for Allah’s blessings in your life.
  6. Mindfulness in Daily Activities: Remind yourself of Allah’s presence during daily tasks by saying “Bismillah” (In the name of Allah) before starting them.

These practices help you maintain a conscious awareness of Allah in your life.
