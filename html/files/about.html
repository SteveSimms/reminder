What is the Reminder?

The Reminder refers to the Quran, which serves as a guidance, warning, and mercy for humanity. It is meant to remind people of Allah’s commands, the consequences of their actions, and the importance of faith and worship. It emphasizes reflection, mindfulness, and the significance of adhering to divine teachings.

What is the Quran?

The Quran is the holy book of Islam, believed to be the word of Allah (God) as revealed to the Prophet Muhammad through the angel Gabriel. It consists of verses (ayahs) organized into chapters (surahs) and serves as a guide for faith, practice, and morality for Muslims. The Quran is recited in Arabic and is considered a source of spiritual guidance and law.

What is the Hadith?

A Hadith is a recorded saying, action, or approval of the Prophet Muhammad. It serves as a source of Islamic law and guidance, complementing the Quran. Hadiths are considered vital for understanding the teachings and practices of Islam.

Who is Allah?

Allah is the one and only God in Islam, the Creator of all things, the Most Compassionate, the Most Merciful, and the Supreme Being who maintains the universe. He possesses the Most Beautiful Names, is All-Knowing, and is both the Sustainer and Judge of all creation.

Who is the prophet Muhammad

Prophet Muhammad is regarded as the last prophet in Islam. He is known as Allah’s messenger who brought the final revelation, the Quran, to humanity. Muhammad is celebrated for his exemplary character, compassion, and leadership, and he is recognized as “the Seal of the Prophets,” meaning no other prophets will come after him. His teachings emphasize monotheism, moral conduct, and the importance of community.

Why do we ‘worship’ Allah?

We worship Allah because He is the Creator of all things, the Sustainer of life, and is deserving of reverence and devotion. Worshiping Allah is an acknowledgment of His greatness, mercy, and authority, as well as a means to seek guidance, forgiveness, and fulfillment in life. It reflects gratitude for His blessings and an understanding of our purpose as His creations.

How do we ‘worship’ Allah?

Worshiping Allah involves sincere devotion, prayer, supplication, and obedience to His commandments. It includes acknowledging His oneness, performing the five daily prayers, giving charity (Zakat), fasting during Ramadan, and living a life in accordance with His teachings. Additionally, one should express gratitude, seek forgiveness, and strive to do good deeds, always with the intention of pleasing Allah.

What happens when we die?

When we die, our souls are taken by the Angel of Death, and we are returned to our Lord for judgment. After death, we may either enter Paradise or Hell based on our deeds and faith. The body will decay, but it will be resurrected on the Day of Judgment when all souls will be brought back to life for accountability.

How do I remember Allah?

You can remember Allah by engaging in regular prayers, reciting the Quran, reflecting on His creations, and expressing gratitude for His blessings. Additionally, dedicating time for dhikr (remembrance) through phrases like “SubhanAllah” (Glory be to Allah), “Alhamdulillah” (All praise is due to Allah), and “Allahu Akbar” (Allah is the Greatest) can deepen your connection with Him.

How do I become Muslim?

To become Muslim, you need to declare the Shahada, which is the testimony of faith. You say sincerely: “Ashhadu an la ilaha illallah, wa ashhadu anna Muhammadur-Rasulullah,” meaning “I bear witness that there is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is His messenger.” You can also perform a ritual washing (Ghusl) and begin to learn about the tenets of Islam, including the Five Pillars: belief in Allah and His Messenger, prayer, fasting during Ramadan, giving Zakat (charity), and performing Hajj (pilgrimage) if possible. It is often helpful to connect with a local Muslim community for support and guidance.
