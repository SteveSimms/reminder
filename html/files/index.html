What is the Reminder?

The Reminder refers to guidance and teachings revealed by Allah, often embodied in the Quran and the messages of the Prophets. It serves as a warning and a source of guidance for humanity, encouraging reflection, mindfulness, and adherence to faith. The Reminder aims to benefit those who are receptive and mindful of Allah’s signs and revelations.

What is the Quran?

The Quran is the holy book of Islam, considered the literal word of God (Allah) as revealed to the Prophet Muhammad through the angel Gabriel. It serves as a guide for various aspects of life, including spiritual, moral, and legal matters. The Quran is written in Arabic and is known for its eloquent language and profound teachings.

What is the Hadith?

A Hadith is a recorded saying, action, or approval of the Prophet Muhammad. It serves as a source of guidance for Muslims in matters of faith, law, and ethics, complementing the teachings of the Quran.

Who is Allah?

Allah is the one true God in Islam, recognized as the Creator of all things, the Most Compassionate, the Most Merciful, and the Supreme Being who sustains and maintains the universe. He is unique, with no partners or equals, and is characterized by His many beautiful names and attributes. Allah is also described as All-Knowing, All-Wise, and the ultimate source of guidance and wisdom.

Who is the prophet Muhammad

Prophet Muhammad is considered the final messenger of Allah in Islam, known for delivering the message of the Quran and guiding the people towards the path of monotheism. He is referred to as “Al-Mutawakkil” (one who depends upon Allah) and is recognized for his character marked by kindness, forgiveness, and humility. Muhammad is seen as a witness, a giver of glad tidings, and a warner, as outlined in Islamic texts. He is believed to have received divine revelation and is regarded as an exemplary figure in the Muslim faith.

Why do we ‘worship’ Allah?

We worship Allah as an expression of our gratitude, reverence, and obedience to Him. Worshiping Allah acknowledges His sovereignty, power, and the blessings He provides. It is a means to seek guidance, forgiveness, and spiritual fulfillment, aligning ourselves with the purpose of our creation and cultivating a relationship with our Creator.

How do we ‘worship’ Allah?

Worshiping Allah involves sincere devotion and obedience to Him. This can be expressed through prayer, acts of charity, remembrance, and following His commandments. Core practices include performing the five daily prayers, reading and reflecting on the Quran, being grateful, and maintaining good character and ethics in daily life. It’s about establishing a personal connection with Allah and living in accordance with Islamic teachings.

What happens when we die?

When we die, our souls are taken by the Angel of Death, and we return to Allah for judgment. Our deeds are recorded, and we will be held accountable for them on the Day of Resurrection, when everyone will be resurrected from their graves and face their final fate, either in Paradise or Hell.

How do I remember Allah?

You can remember Allah by engaging in various acts of worship and reflection, such as:

  1. Performing regular prayers (Salah) to remember Him at prescribed times.
  2. Reciting the Quran and reflecting upon its meanings.
  3. Engaging in dhikr (remembrance) by repeating phrases like “SubhanAllah” (Glory be to Allah), “Alhamdulillah” (Praise be to Allah), and “Allahu Akbar” (Allah is the Greatest).
  4. Reflecting on the creation and blessings of Allah, and expressing gratitude.
  5. Seeking knowledge about Him through studying His signs and attributes.
  6. Making supplications (duas) with sincerity and humility.

Consistently incorporating these practices into your daily life will help keep you mindful of Allah.

How do I become Muslim?

To become a Muslim, you need to declare your faith by saying the Shahada, which is the testimony of faith. You should say, “Ashhadu an la ilaha illa Allah, wahdahu la sharika lahu, wa ashhadu anna Muhammadur Rasul Allah,” meaning “I bear witness that there is no deity but Allah, and Muhammad is His Messenger.”

After declaring the Shahada, it is recommended to perform the following steps:

  1. Learn about the fundamental beliefs of Islam, such as belief in the oneness of God (Allah), the prophets, the holy books, angels, the Day of Judgment, and divine decrees.
  2. Begin practicing the Five Pillars of Islam, which are:
    • Performing the five daily prayers (Salah).
    • Giving to charity (Zakat).
    • Fasting during the month of Ramadan (Sawm).
    • Making the pilgrimage to Mecca (Hajj) if possible.
    • Declaring the Shahada again as an affirmation of your faith.
  3. Connect with a local Muslim community for guidance and support in your journey.

It’s important to approach this journey with sincerity and openness, seeking knowledge and understanding of Islam.
