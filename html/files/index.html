What is the Reminder?

The Reminder refers to the Quran, which serves as guidance, a warning, and a mercy for humanity. It is a text that encourages reflection, worship, and mindfulness of Allah, reminding believers of His teachings and the consequences of their actions.

What is the Quran?

The Quran is the holy book of Islam, believed by Muslims to be the word of Allah (God) as revealed to the Prophet Muhammad through the Angel Gabriel. It serves as a guide for personal conduct, legal matters, and spiritual beliefs, and is recited and memorized by Muslims around the world. The Quran is written in Arabic and is considered to be a complete and final revelation, containing teachings on morality, worship, and law.

What is the Hadith?

A Hadith is a record of the sayings, actions, or approvals of the Prophet Muhammad. It serves as a source of guidance for Muslims, providing context for Islamic teachings and laws alongside the Quran.

Who is Allah?

Allah is the One true God in Islam, the Creator of all things, and the Sustainer of the heavens and the earth. He is described as All-Knowing, All-Wise, the Most Compassionate, and the Most Merciful. Allah holds the most beautiful names and is the only one worthy of worship.

Who is the prophet Muhammad

Prophet Muhammad is regarded in Islam as the final messenger of Allah (God) and is known as Al-Mutawakkil (the one who depends on Allah). He was sent as a witness, a bringer of glad tidings, and a warner to humanity. Muhammad conveyed the message of Islam and is considered the last prophet, succeeding other prophets like Moses and Jesus. His teachings and life are recorded in the Quran and Hadiths, serving as a guide for Muslims around the world.

Why do we ‘worship’ Allah?

We worship Allah to acknowledge His greatness, express gratitude for His blessings, seek guidance, and fulfill our purpose as His creation. Worship is an act of devotion that reinforces our relationship with Allah and aligns us with His will.

How do we ‘worship’ Allah?

Worshiping Allah involves sincere devotion and submission to Him alone. This includes fulfilling the five pillars of Islam: declaring the Shahada (faith), performing the five daily prayers (Salah), giving alms (Zakat), fasting during Ramadan, and making the pilgrimage to Mecca (Hajj) if able. Additionally, worship encompasses acts of kindness, prayer, remembrance of Allah (Dhikr), and following His guidance in daily life.

What happens when we die?

When we die, our souls are taken by the Angel of Death, and we return to Allah for judgment. Each person will be shown their place in the Hereafter, either in Paradise or Hell. Our deeds in this world determine our fate in the afterlife. Ultimately, all will be resurrected for the Day of Judgment.

How do I become Muslim?

To become Muslim, you need to embrace the faith by sincerely declaring the Shahada, which is the testimony of faith. This involves saying: “Ashhadu an la ilaha illallah, wa ashhadu anna Muhammadur Rasulullah,” which means “I bear witness that there is no deity but Allah, and Muhammad is His messenger.”

Additionally, it’s beneficial to learn about the fundamental beliefs and practices of Islam, including the Five Pillars: the declaration of faith (Shahada), prayer (Salah), giving charity (Zakat), fasting during Ramadan (Sawm), and pilgrimage to Mecca (Hajj) if able. Seeking guidance from knowledgeable Muslims or resources from a local mosque can also provide support as you begin your journey in Islam.
