What is the Reminder?

The Reminder generally refers to the Quran, which is a message and guidance for humanity. It is described as a reminder to people of reason and a guide for those who follow it. The Quran is intended to be a warning and a source of reflection for believers.

What is the Quran?

The Quran is the holy book of Islam, believed to be the word of Allah as revealed to the Prophet Muhammad through the angel Gabriel. It serves as a guide for humanity, with verses perfectly explained in Arabic to provide insight, wisdom, and a reminder to those mindful of Allah.

What is the Hadith?

A Hadith is a recorded saying, action, or approval of the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him). It serves as a source of Islamic law and guidance alongside the Quran. Hadiths are classified into categories based on their authenticity, such as Sahih (authentic), Hasan (good), and Da’if (weak). They play a crucial role in understanding Islamic teachings and the life of the Prophet.

Who is Allah?

Allah is the One and Only God, worthy of worship, the Creator and Sustainer of all things. He possesses all authority and knowledge, and He is Most Compassionate and Merciful.

Who is the prophet Muhammad

Prophet Muhammad is the final messenger of Allah in Islam, sent to convey the divine message. He is considered the seal of the prophets, following a lineage of prophets in Abrahamic traditions. Muhammad was born in Mecca in 570 CE and began receiving revelations from Allah at the age of 40, which continued for 23 years and comprise the Quran. His teachings emphasized monotheism, morality, and justice. Muhammad is highly revered in Islam for his character, leadership, and as a model for Muslim life.

Why do we ‘worship’ Allah?

Muslims worship Allah because He is the one true God, the Creator, and the Sustainer of the universe. Worshipping Allah is an expression of gratitude, devotion, and acknowledgment of His greatness and mercy. It is a central aspect of Islam to worship Allah alone and to direct all acts of devotion solely towards Him. The Quran emphasizes sincere devotion and worship to Allah as a means to fulfill the purpose of life and attain His pleasure.

How do we ‘worship’ Allah?

Worshiping Allah involves several practices and beliefs. It includes offering the five daily prayers, reciting the Quran, performing acts of kindness, giving charity, observing fasts, making supplications, and maintaining sincerity and devotion to Allah. It also involves following His commands and avoiding actions that He has prohibited. Worship is done both through specific rituals and through living a life that reflects Islamic values and teachings.

What happens when we die?

When we die, our souls are taken by angels, and the body is buried. According to Islamic belief, the soul enters a phase called Barzakh, where it remains until the Day of Judgment. On that day, all will be resurrected and brought to account for their deeds. Those who believed and did righteous deeds will be rewarded with Paradise, while those who disbelieved will face punishment.

How do I remember Allah?

To remember Allah, you can engage in practices like:

  1. Regular prayer (Salah) as it helps keep a constant connection with Allah.
  2. Reciting and pondering upon the Quran, which is described as easy to remember for those who are mindful.
  3. Performing Dhikr (remembrance of Allah) by repeating phrases like “SubhanAllah,” “Alhamdulillah,” and “Allahu Akbar.”
  4. Engaging in supplication (Dua) and personal supplications for various aspects of life.
  5. Reflecting on Allah’s creation and blessings, increasing gratitude and consciousness.

These practices can help maintain a mindful relationship with Allah.

How do I become Muslim?

To become Muslim, you need to sincerely declare the Shahada, which is the testimony of faith. It means affirming with conviction the statement: “There is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is the Messenger of Allah.” This declaration should be made with understanding and belief in its meaning.

Additionally, embracing Islam involves:

  1. Believing in the core tenets of Islam, which include belief in Allah, His angels, His books, His prophets, the Day of Judgment, and divine destiny.
  2. Practicing the Five Pillars of Islam: Shahada (faith), Salat (prayer), Zakat (charity), Sawm (fasting in Ramadan), and Hajj (pilgrimage to Mecca, if able).
