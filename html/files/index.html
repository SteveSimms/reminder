What is the Reminder?

The Reminder refers to divine guidance and teachings revealed by Allah, aimed at reminding humanity about the truth, morality, and the consequences of their actions. It is often associated with the Quran, which serves as a guide and a source of wisdom for believers.

What is the Quran?

The Quran is the holy book of Islam, believed to be the word of Allah as revealed to the Prophet Muhammad through the angel Gabriel. It serves as a guide for Muslims, containing teachings on faith, worship, morality, and law. The Quran is written in Arabic and is composed of verses (ayahs) organized into chapters (surahs). It is considered a complete and final revelation from Allah to humanity.

What is the Hadith?

A Hadith is a recorded saying, action, or tacit approval of the Prophet Muhammad. It serves as a significant source of guidance for Muslims, alongside the Quran, covering various aspects of life, law, and morality.

Who is Allah?

Allah is the one and only God in Islam, the Creator of all things, and the Sustainer of the universe. He is described as the Most Compassionate, Most Merciful, and possesses the Most Beautiful Names. Allah is also characterized by His attributes such as being All-Wise, All-Knowing, and All-Powerful. Believers are encouraged to worship Him alone.

Who is the prophet Muhammad

Prophet Muhammad is considered the last prophet in Islam, sent by Allah to guide humanity. He is known for his teachings, which form the foundation of the Islamic faith, and is regarded as a witness, bearer of glad tidings, and a warner. He preached the oneness of Allah and conveyed the message of the Quran over his lifetime. Muhammad is characterized by his merciful and compassionate nature, and his followers believe he exemplified the best of moral conduct.

Why do we ‘worship’ Allah?

We worship Allah to acknowledge His uniqueness, sovereignty, and authority as the Creator of all things. Worship is an expression of gratitude, devotion, and submission to the One who provides for and sustains us. It fulfills our purpose in life, aligns us with divine guidance, and strengthens our connection with Allah. Ultimately, worship helps us to recognize our dependence on Him and to seek His mercy and forgiveness.

How do we ‘worship’ Allah?

Worshiping Allah involves sincerity in devotion, performing prescribed acts of worship such as prayer, fasting, paying alms (Zakat), and following the teachings of the Quran and the Prophet Muhammad. It also includes remembering Allah through supplication, gratitude, and striving to live a life in accordance with His guidance. The essence of worship is recognizing His oneness and submitting to His will.

What happens when we die?

When we die, our souls are taken by the Angel of Death, and we experience a transition to the afterlife. Each person will be resurrected on the Day of Judgment, where their deeds will be assessed, leading to either eternal reward in Paradise or punishment in Hell. The state of a person’s soul after death can reflect their life choices, and they may be shown their place in the afterlife before the final resurrection.

How do I remember Allah?

You can remember Allah by engaging in regular prayers, reciting the Quran, making supplications (duas), reflecting on His signs in creation, and being mindful of His presence in your daily actions. Incorporating remembrance into your routine, such as saying phrases like “SubhanAllah” (Glory be to Allah), “Alhamdulillah” (Praise be to Allah), and “Allahu Akbar” (Allah is the Greatest), can also help in keeping Allah in your thoughts.

How do I become Muslim?

To become a Muslim, you need to sincerely declare the Shahada, the Islamic declaration of faith. This involves saying: “Ashhadu an la ilaha illallah, wa ashhadu anna Muhammadur Rasulullah,” which means “I bear witness that there is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is His Messenger.” After declaring this with conviction in your heart, it is recommended to perform the obligatory acts of worship such as prayer (Salah) and fasting during Ramadan. Seeking knowledge about Islam and joining a community of believers for support is also encouraged.
