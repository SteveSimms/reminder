What is the Reminder?

The Reminder refers to the revelations and guidance provided by Allah, primarily through the Quran. It serves as a warning, lesson, and a source of guidance for humanity, encouraging reflection, mindfulness, and faith.

What is the Quran?

The Quran is the holy book of Islam, believed to be the word of Allah revealed to the Prophet Muhammad through the Angel Gabriel. It serves as a guide for personal conduct, spirituality, law, and ethics for Muslims, and is written in Arabic. The Quran is regarded as a complete and final revelation, embodying clear guidance and wisdom for humanity.

Who is Allah?

Allah is the one true God in Islam, believed to be the Creator of all things. He is described as the Most Compassionate, Most Merciful, and has many beautiful names that reflect His attributes, including the Almighty, the All-Wise, and the Sustainer. Allah is unique, having no partners or offspring, and is the supreme authority to whom all creation is ultimately accountable.

Who is the prophet Muhammad

Prophet Muhammad is regarded as the last prophet in Islam, sent by Allah as a messenger to guide humanity to the truth. He is known as “Al-Mutawakkil” (one who depends upon Allah) and is characterized as compassionate, forgiving, and humble. He received divine revelation, which is recorded in the Quran, and he delivered the message of monotheism, urging people to worship only Allah. His teachings emphasize good character, kindness, and justice.

Why do we ‘worship’ Allah?

We worship Allah as an expression of our devotion, gratitude, and acknowledgment of His oneness, greatness, and authority. Worship is a means of fostering a relationship with our Creator, seeking guidance, and fulfilling our purpose in life while recognizing His mercy and power over all existence.

How do we ‘worship’ Allah?

Worshiping Allah involves sincere devotion and submission to Him alone. This can be expressed through various acts such as:

  1. Prayer: Performing the five obligatory prayers (Salah) with concentration and humility.
  2. Supplication: Making personal prayers (Dua) to seek guidance and help.
  3. Reading the Quran: Engaging with the holy text for guidance and reflection.
  4. Giving Charity: Performing acts of kindness and supporting those in need through Zakat and other forms of charity.
  5. Fasting: Observing fasting during Ramadan as a means of self-discipline and spiritual growth.
  6. Remembrance: Engaging in the remembrance of Allah (Dhikr) through phrases that glorify Him.
  7. Living Righteously: Following ethical guidelines and commands set forth in Islam to lead a life in accordance with His will.

All these acts should be performed with sincerity, intention, and an awareness that Allah is ever-present and all-knowing.

What happens when we die?

When we die, our souls are taken by the Angel of Death, and we will eventually be brought back to life on the Day of Judgment. In the interim, our deeds will follow us and we will be judged based on them. Believers will be rewarded, while disbelievers may face punishment. The grave is a place where, according to some beliefs, the deceased may be questioned about their faith and deeds. Ultimately, everyone will face resurrection and accountability before Allah.

How do I remember Allah?

You can remember Allah through various practices such as:

  1. Regular Prayer (Salah): Establish your daily prayers, as they are a direct means of remembering Allah.
  2. Recitation of the Quran: Read and reflect upon the Quran, which serves as a reminder of Allah’s words and guidance.
  3. Dhikr (Remembrance): Engage in Dhikr by repeating phrases praising Allah, such as “SubhanAllah” (Glory be to Allah), “Alhamdulillah” (All praise is for Allah), and “Allahu Akbar” (Allah is the Greatest).
  4. Gratitude: Be mindful of Allah’s blessings and express gratitude for them in your daily life.
  5. Mindfulness in Actions: Remember Allah during your daily activities by consciously striving to align your actions with His guidance.
  6. Seeking Knowledge: Learn about Allah’s attributes and the teachings of His Messenger to deepen your connection.

Incorporating these practices into your life will help you remember Allah consistently.

How do I become Muslim?

To become a Muslim, you need to declare the Shahada, which is the testimony of faith. This involves saying, “Ashhadu an la ilaha illallah, wa ashhadu anna Muhammadur Rasoolullah,” meaning “I bear witness that there is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is His Messenger.” It is recommended to do this with a sincere heart, understanding its meaning. After that, you may want to learn about the basic tenets of Islam, such as the Five Pillars, and start practicing them, including prayer, fasting, and giving charity. It can also be beneficial to connect with a local Muslim community or mosque for guidance and support.
